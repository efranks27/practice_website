About Me

My name is Ethan Franks and I am a Network Technology student at Carleton University in Ottawa, Canada. I've always had a keen interest in technology and always wanted the newest devices. When I was applying to programs in grade 12, I still wasn't totally set on what I wanted to do. All I knew was that I wanted to take something to do with technology.

I was pretty set on going to school in Ottawa because being from the Kingston area, Ottawa was far enough away from my parents that I'd be on my own, but not too far that I couldn't visit. I started looking through all the programs Carleton had to offer and once I clicked on the "Network Technology" link, I was fairly set on the program.

I lived in a rural town north of Kingston so we never had good internet availability. I didn't even have wifi at my dad's house until it was required for my online university classes. Because of this, and having slow internet at my mom's house, I was very interested in how I could increase internet availability and reduce latency and packet loss I always experienced when trying to play games.
